Goalkeeper!

A goalkeeper is a player whose duty it is to defend his goal , that is, to prevent the opposing team from scoring a goal . There are usually special rules that only apply to goalkeepers. Goalkeepers often wear additional equipment. In football, the goalkeeper may touch the ball with his hands within his own penalty area . The goalkeeper's uniform must be different from that of the other players on the team, as well as the opponents and referees. The goalkeeper wears special gloves to cushion the blows and allow him to better grip the ball. A goalkeeper who leaves the penalty area is considered a regular player.

The goalkeeper must be athletic, have jumping ability, which provides him with special advantages when playing in the penalty area, be agile, strong, agile like an acrobat, be able to get ahead of the opponent, stop and catch the ball in any position or hit it with his fists. He must have a strong kick and strong throw, be able to throw the ball over the bar or send it past the side post.

In ice hockey, the goalkeeper wears special equipment consisting of a reinforced carapace, helmet, shin guards, a trap, a pancake and a special stick . The goalkeeper can push the puck to the ice or trap it to stop play by holding the puck for three seconds. Attacking the goalkeeper is considered a violation. Unlike goalkeepers in other sports, a goaltender in English ice hockey is not usually called a goalkeeper, but a goaltender or goalie.
